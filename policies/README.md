## Policy Development Methodology

All policies follow a consistent structure aligned with GRC best practices:

**1. Document Control**
- Clear ownership, versioning, and review schedules
- Classification and parent policy hierarchy

**2. Purpose and Scope**
- Explicit statement of policy intent and applicability
- Boundaries clearly defined

**3. Policy Statement**
- Core requirements and standards
- Specific, measurable, and enforceable provisions

**4. Roles and Responsibilities**
- Clear accountability assignments
- Separation of duties where appropriate

**5. Procedures**
- Step-by-step implementation guidance
- Operational workflows and decision points

**6. Compliance and Enforcement**
- Monitoring mechanisms and audit requirements
- Consequences for non-compliance

**7. Framework Alignment**
- Explicit mapping to recognized security standards
- Control objective traceability

**8. Organizational Benefits**
- Business value articulation
- Risk reduction and efficiency gains

**9. Evidence of Compliance**
- Audit trail requirements
- Documentation and reporting cadence

## Framework Coverage

These policies collectively address requirements from:

**NIST Cybersecurity Framework v1.1**
- Identify (ID): Asset management, risk assessment, governance
- Protect (PR): Access control, data security, protective technology
- Detect (DE): Security monitoring and event detection
- Respond (RS): Incident response planning and communications
- Recover (RC): Recovery planning and improvements

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022**
- Organizational controls (A.5): Information security policies, roles, asset management
- People controls (A.6): Screening, awareness, disciplinary process
- Physical controls (A.7): Physical security and environmental controls
- Technological controls (A.8): Access control, cryptography, logging

**NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)**
- Prepare, Categorize, Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, Monitor

**COSO Enterprise Risk Management**
- Governance and Culture, Strategy and Objective-Setting, Performance, Review and Revision

**ISO 22301 Business Continuity**
- Business impact analysis, continuity planning, testing, and exercises

## Policy Maintenance

**Review Cycle:** All policies are reviewed annually or when significant organizational changes occur.

**Update Process:** Policy updates follow formal change management with:
1. Risk assessment of proposed changes
2. Stakeholder review and feedback
3. Approval by policy owner and Risk Management Committee
4. Communication to affected parties
5. Training updates as needed

**Version Control:** Each policy maintains a document history table tracking all revisions.
