## Description

**Purpose:** Provides a framework for detecting, reporting, and responding to information security incidents.

**Importance:** Ensures timely and effective response to security incidents, minimizing impact.

**Relevant Frameworks:** ISO/IEC 27035, NIST SP 800-61, and GDPR

---

## A. Purpose

To establish a structured and consistent approach to managing information security incidents within Emyzer Technology. This policy aims to minimize the impact of incidents on operations, protect sensitive information, and ensure compliance with ISO/IEC 27035, NIST SP 800-61, and GDPR frameworks.

---

## B. Scope

This policy applies to all departments and activities within Emyzer Technology, including but not limited to:

- Information Technology
- Human Resources
- Legal
- Finance
- Operations
- All third-party vendors and contractors handling Emyzer Technology's information assets

It encompasses all information systems, applications, networks, and data repositories where Emyzer Technology conducts its business.

## D. Policy Statement

Emyzer Technology is committed to promptly identifying, reporting, and responding to information security incidents. The organization will:

1. **Establish and maintain an Incident Response Team (IRT)** with clearly defined roles and responsibilities.
2. **Implement incident detection capabilities** to identify security events in real time.
3. **Follow structured incident response procedures** aligned with ISO/IEC 27035 and NIST SP 800-61.
4. **Comply with GDPR breach notification requirements** for incidents involving personal data.
5. **Conduct post-incident reviews** to drive continuous improvement in security posture.

---

## E. Roles and Responsibilities

### Information Security Officer (ISO)
- Oversee the incident management process
- Ensure compliance with relevant standards and regulations
- Report significant incidents to executive management

### Incident Response Team (IRT)
- Manage detection, analysis, containment, eradication, and recovery of incidents
- Coordinate response activities across departments
- Maintain incident documentation and evidence

### Department Heads
- Ensure teams comply with the incident management policy
- Support incident response activities within their areas of responsibility
- Participate in post-incident reviews

### All Employees
- Report any suspected or actual information security incidents immediately to the ISO or IRT
- Preserve evidence and avoid actions that could worsen the incident
- Cooperate fully with incident response activities

---

## F. Procedures

### F.1 Incident Identification and Reporting
All employees must report suspected incidents to the IRT immediately through established reporting channels. The IRT logs the incident in the incident management system with initial classification and severity assessment.

### F.2 Incident Analysis
The IRT conducts a preliminary analysis to determine the scope, impact, and classification of the incident. If personal data is involved, GDPR breach notification procedures are initiated immediately.

### F.3 Incident Classification
Incidents are classified by severity (Critical, High, Medium, Low) based on impact to confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets.

### F.4 Containment
The IRT implements immediate measures to contain the incident and prevent further damage. Containment strategies vary based on incident type and severity.

### F.5 Eradication
The IRT identifies and removes the root cause of the incident. This may include patching vulnerabilities, removing malware, or closing unauthorized access points.

### F.6 Recovery
The IRT restores affected systems and services to normal operation. Recovery procedures include verification of system integrity and monitoring for recurrence.

### F.7 Post-Incident Review
A structured post-incident review is conducted to identify lessons learned, update procedures, and improve future incident response efforts. Findings are documented and shared with relevant stakeholders.

## G. Compliance and Monitoring

### Internal Audits
The Information Security Officer monitors compliance with this policy through regular audits and incident reviews. Audit findings are reported to executive management.

### Performance Metrics
Key performance indicators (KPIs) track incident response effectiveness, including:
- Time to detect incidents
- Time to contain incidents
- Time to recover from incidents
- Number of incidents by category and severity

### Enforcement
Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action including termination of employment or contract.
