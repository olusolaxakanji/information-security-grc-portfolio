## A. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a comprehensive risk management framework for Emyzer Technology that integrates the principles of NIST SP 800-37 (Risk Management Framework), COSO ERM (Enterprise Risk Management â€“ Integrated Framework), and CIS RAM (Center for Internet Security Risk Assessment Method). This policy aims to identify, assess, manage, and mitigate risks that could impact the organization's ability to achieve its objectives.

---

## B. Scope

This policy applies to all departments and activities within Emyzer Technology, including but not limited to Information Technology, Human Resources, Finance, Operations, and Legal. It encompasses all types of risks, including strategic, operational, financial, compliance, and information security.

---

## C. Definitions

**Risk:** The potential for loss or harm to an organization's assets, operations, or reputation.

**Risk Management Framework (RMF):** A structured process to identify and manage risks.

**COSO ERM:** A framework for identifying, assessing, and managing risks across an enterprise.

**CIS RAM:** A methodology for assessing and managing information security risks.

**Risk Assessment:** Identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risks.

**Mitigation:** Actions taken to reduce the likelihood or impact of a risk.

---

## D. Policy Statement

Emyzer Technology is committed to managing risks proactively and systematically to protect its assets, ensure the continuity of operations, and maintain compliance with applicable laws and standards. The organization will implement a risk management framework incorporating NIST SP 800-37, COSO ERM, and CIS RAM to guide risk identification, assessment, mitigation, monitoring, and reporting.

---

## E. Roles and Responsibilities

**Risk Management Committee:** Oversees the risk management program, approves policies and procedures, and reviews risk assessments and mitigation plans.

**Chief Risk Officer (CRO):** Leads the risk management efforts, ensures the integration of risk management into business processes, and reports to senior management and the board on risk-related issues.

**Department Heads:** Identify and manage risks within their areas of responsibility, ensuring that risk management activities are aligned with the overall risk management framework.

**Risk Owners:** Individuals assigned to manage specific risks, responsible for implementing and monitoring mitigation plans.

**All Employees:** Understand and comply with the risk management policy, report identified risks, and participate in risk management activities as required.

---

## F. Procedures

**1. Risk Identification:** Identify risks using a combination of risk assessments, audits, incident reports, and employee feedback.

**2. Risk Assessment:** Assess identified risks using qualitative and quantitative methods, considering the likelihood and impact of each risk.

**3. Risk Mitigation:** Develop and implement risk mitigation plans to reduce the likelihood or impact of risks to acceptable levels.

**4. Risk Monitoring:** Continuously monitor risks and the effectiveness of mitigation measures, adjusting plans as necessary.

**5. Risk Reporting:** Regularly report on the status of risks and mitigation efforts to senior management and the Risk Management Committee.

---

## G. Compliance and Monitoring

Compliance with this policy will be monitored through regular audits, reviews of risk management activities, and performance metrics. Non-compliance will be addressed through corrective actions and, if necessary, disciplinary measures.

## Framework Alignment

This policy addresses the following framework requirements:

**NIST SP 800-37 Rev. 2 (Risk Management Framework):**
- Prepare: Essential activities to prepare the organization for risk management
- Categorize: Categorize systems and information based on impact analysis
- Select: Select appropriate security controls based on risk assessment
- Implement: Implement security controls and document implementation
- Assess: Assess security controls to determine effectiveness
- Authorize: Make risk-based authorization decisions
- Monitor: Continuously monitor security controls and organizational risk

**COSO ERM Framework (2017):**
- Governance and Culture: Exercise board risk oversight and establish operating structures
- Strategy and Objective-Setting: Analyze business context and define risk appetite
- Performance: Identify and assess risks, prioritize and respond to risks
- Review and Revision: Assess substantial change and review risk and performance
- Information, Communication, and Reporting: Leverage information systems and communicate risk information

**CIS Risk Assessment Method (RAM):**
- Asset identification and valuation
- Threat identification and analysis
- Vulnerability assessment
- Impact analysis and risk calculation
- Risk treatment and mitigation planning

**ISO 31000:2018 (Risk Management Guidelines):**
- Integration of risk management into organizational processes
- Structured and comprehensive approach to risk management
- Customization of risk management framework
- Continual improvement through learning and experience

## Organizational Benefits

**Strategic Alignment:** Links risk management to organizational objectives, ensuring resources are allocated to highest-priority risks.

**Informed Decision-Making:** Provides leadership with comprehensive risk intelligence for strategic and operational decisions.

**Regulatory Compliance:** Demonstrates proactive risk management to regulators and auditors, reducing compliance costs and penalties.

**Operational Resilience:** Identifies and mitigates operational vulnerabilities before they result in disruptions or losses.

**Stakeholder Confidence:** Transparent risk management processes build trust with investors, customers, and partners.

**Resource Optimization:** Prioritizes risk mitigation investments based on likelihood and impact analysis, maximizing return on security spending.

**Competitive Advantage:** Mature risk management capabilities differentiate the organization in the marketplace and enable pursuit of higher-value opportunities.
