## A. Purpose

To establish a comprehensive risk management framework for Emyzer Technology that integrates the principles of NIST SP 800-37 (Risk Management Framework), COSO ERM (Enterprise Risk Management – Integrated Framework), and CIS RAM (Center for Internet Security Risk Assessment Method). This policy aims to identify, assess, manage, and mitigate risks that could impact the organization's ability to achieve its objectives.

---

## B. Scope

This policy applies to all departments and activities within Emyzer Technology, including but not limited to Information Technology, Human Resources, Finance, Operations, Legal, and all third-party vendors and contractors. It encompasses all types of risks, including strategic, operational, financial, compliance, and information security.

**Third-party applicability:** Third parties handling organizational data or systems shall meet equivalent risk management controls via contractual requirements and periodic security reviews.

## C. Definitions

1. **Risk:** The potential for loss or harm to an organization's assets, operations, or reputation.

2. **Risk Management Framework (RMF):** A structured process to identify and manage risks through systematic categorization, control selection, and continuous monitoring.

3. **COSO ERM:** A framework for identifying, assessing, and managing risks across an enterprise to support strategic objectives.

4. **CIS RAM:** A methodology for assessing and managing information security risks using standardized assessment techniques.

5. **Risk Assessment:** The process of identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risks to determine their potential impact and likelihood.

6. **Risk Mitigation:** Actions taken to reduce the likelihood or impact of a risk to acceptable levels.

7. **Risk Appetite:** The amount and type of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives.

8. **Risk Tolerance:** The specific maximum risk an organization is willing to take regarding specific objectives.

9. **Inherent Risk:** The level of risk before controls are applied.

10. **Residual Risk:** The level of risk remaining after controls are implemented.

## D. Policy Statement

Emyzer Technology is committed to managing risks proactively and systematically to protect its assets, ensure the continuity of operations, and maintain compliance with applicable laws and standards. To achieve this, the organization shall:

1. Implement a risk management framework incorporating NIST SP 800-37, COSO ERM, and CIS RAM principles.

2. Conduct regular risk assessments **quarterly**, with critical risks reviewed **monthly** for status, treatment progress, and risk acceptance decisions.

3. Establish clear risk appetite and tolerance levels aligned with strategic business goals.

4. Assign risk ownership with accountability for risk mitigation and monitoring.

5. Integrate risk management into all business processes and decision-making activities through documented governance procedures.

6. Monitor and report on risk posture to executive management and the board **monthly**.

7. Review and audit the risk management program **annually** with continuous improvement through management review meetings held **quarterly**.

## E. Roles and Responsibilities

### E.1 Risk Management Committee
Shall oversee the risk management program and approve risk management policies and procedures. Shall review risk assessments and mitigation plans **quarterly**. Shall establish organizational risk appetite and tolerance through **annual risk appetite statement review**. Shall serve as the escalation point for risks exceeding tolerance thresholds.

### E.2 Chief Risk Officer (CRO)
Shall lead risk management efforts across the organization under executive leadership direction. Shall ensure integration of risk management into business processes through documented procedures. Shall report to senior management and the board on risk-related issues **monthly**. Shall coordinate risk assessment activities and approve strategic risk treatment decisions. Acts as the delegated risk executive for day-to-day program operations.

### E.3 Department Heads
Shall identify and manage risks within their areas of responsibility through **quarterly departmental risk reviews**. Shall ensure risk management activities are aligned with the overall risk management framework. Shall support risk owners in implementing mitigation plans. Shall report new or emerging risks within 24 hours of discovery.

### E.4 Risk Owners
Shall manage specific risks assigned to them with documented treatment plans. Shall implement and monitor risk mitigation plans with **monthly status updates**. Shall report on risk status and mitigation effectiveness. Shall escalate issues when risk levels exceed tolerance within 4 hours of identification.

### E.5 All Employees
Shall understand and comply with the risk management policy. Shall complete **annual** risk awareness training and onboarding training within 7 days of start date. Shall report identified risks through established channels within 24 hours of discovery. Shall participate in risk management activities as required and support risk mitigation efforts within their roles.

## F. Minimum Security Requirements

The following requirements establish baseline risk management controls. Detailed implementation procedures are maintained in the **Risk Management Procedures Manual** and related operational documentation.

### F.1 Risk Identification
- Shall identify risks using multiple sources including risk assessments, internal audits, incident reports, employee feedback, industry threat intelligence, and regulatory changes.
- Shall conduct risk identification **continuously** and formally review **quarterly**.
- Shall document all identified risks in the enterprise risk register within 48 hours of identification.
- Shall categorize risks by type (strategic, operational, financial, compliance, information security) with appropriate owner assignment.

### F.2 Risk Assessment
- Shall assess identified risks using both qualitative and quantitative methods **quarterly**.
- Shall consider likelihood of occurrence, potential impact to confidentiality, integrity, and availability, regulatory and compliance implications, and alignment with organizational risk appetite.
- Shall use standardized risk scoring methodology (likelihood × impact) with 5-point scales for consistency.
- Shall document all risk assessments with identified threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, impact, and treatment decisions in the enterprise risk register.

### F.3 Risk Analysis
- Shall analyze risks to determine inherent risk level (before controls), existing control effectiveness, and residual risk level (after controls).
- Shall evaluate interdependencies between risks and cumulative risk exposure.
- Shall complete risk analysis within 5 business days of initial identification for high and critical risks.
- Shall document control effectiveness ratings (Effective, Partially Effective, Ineffective) with supporting evidence.

### F.4 Risk Evaluation
- Shall evaluate risks against established risk criteria and prioritize based on severity.
- Shall assign risk owners within 24 hours of risk evaluation completion.
- Shall make treatment decisions (accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid) with documented rationale.
- Shall escalate risks exceeding tolerance thresholds to the Risk Management Committee within 4 hours.

### F.5 Risk Mitigation
- Shall develop and implement risk mitigation plans to reduce likelihood or impact of risks to acceptable levels.
- Shall include specific actions, timelines, resource requirements, and success metrics in all mitigation plans.
- Shall implement controls for critical risks within 30 days, high risks within 60 days, and moderate risks within 90 days.
- Shall document mitigation plan progress with **monthly status updates** in the GRC platform.

### F.6 Risk Monitoring
- Shall monitor risks and the effectiveness of mitigation measures **continuously**.
- Shall track changes in risk exposure using key risk indicators (KRIs) with **monthly trending analysis**.
- Shall adjust mitigation plans as necessary based on monitoring results.
- Shall maintain risk monitoring dashboards with real-time visibility for executive leadership.

### F.7 Risk Reporting
- Shall provide regular reports on risk status and mitigation efforts to senior management **monthly** and the Risk Management Committee **quarterly**.
- Shall include risk register updates, emerging risk trends, mitigation plan progress, and recommendations for risk treatment in all reports.
- Shall present executive risk dashboard at **monthly** leadership meetings.
- Shall document all risk reporting with distribution lists and acknowledgement records.

## G. Compliance and Monitoring

Compliance with this policy shall be monitored through **quarterly risk management reviews and control assessments** combined with **annual internal audits**. Non-compliance shall be addressed through documented corrective actions within 30 days. Violations may result in access revocation, mandatory corrective training, and disciplinary actions up to and including termination.

### Performance Metrics
Key performance indicators (KPIs) track risk management effectiveness, including:
- Number of risks identified and assessed per quarter
- Percentage of risks with assigned owners and mitigation plans (target: 100%)
- Time to implement risk mitigation measures against SLA targets
- Reduction in residual risk over time
- Compliance with risk assessment schedule (target: 100%)
- Risk acceptance decisions reviewed and approved within established timeframes

## H. Policy Exceptions

### H.1 Exception Request Process
Employees requiring temporary deviation from this policy shall:
1. Submit exception requests via the **ServiceNow GRC/ITSM ticketing workflow (or equivalent ticketing system)** with detailed business justification, risk assessment, and proposed compensating controls.
2. Obtain approval from the Chief Risk Officer for tactical exceptions (≤30 days) or CISO for strategic exceptions (>30 days).
3. Accept exceptions valid for a maximum of **90 days** unless formally renewed.
4. Document compensating controls implemented during the exception period.

### H.2 Exception Governance
- All active exceptions shall be reviewed **quarterly** by the Chief Risk Officer to confirm continued necessity.
- Exception renewals shall require updated business justification and CISO approval.
- Each exception shall include a remediation plan with target closure date not to exceed 180 days from initial approval.
- Exception tracking shall be maintained in the GRC platform with status updates provided to executive leadership **monthly**.

### N.1 Risk Register Evidence
- **Risk Register:** Comprehensive documentation of identified risks maintained in GRC platform, showing risk ID, description, category, owner, likelihood, impact, risk score, controls, treatment decision, and status. Updated **continuously** and formally reviewed **quarterly**. Retained indefinitely (current version with full history).
- **Risk Categorization Records:** Documentation of risk classification by type (strategic, operational, financial, compliance, information security) with owner assignment. Retained for 5 years.
- **Risk Scoring Documentation:** Standardized methodology application with likelihood and impact ratings, including rationale for scores. Retained for 5 years.

### N.2 Risk Assessment Evidence
- **Risk Assessment Reports:** Formal risk assessments using NIST RMF, COSO ERM, and CIS RAM methodologies showing assessment scope, methodology, findings, and recommendations. Completed **quarterly**. Retained for 5 years.
- **Threat Intelligence Reports:** Documentation of external threat landscape analysis informing risk assessments. Retained for 3 years.
- **Vulnerability Assessment Results:** Technical vulnerability findings correlated to risk register entries. Retained for 3 years.
- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** Annual assessment of critical business processes, dependencies, and impact ratings. Retained for 5 years.

### N.3 Risk Committee Evidence
- **Risk Committee Minutes:** Documentation of Risk Management Committee meetings including attendees, agenda items, decisions, approvals, and action items. Meetings held **quarterly**. Retained for 7 years.
- **Risk Appetite Statement:** Formal documentation of organizational risk appetite and tolerance levels approved by executive leadership. Reviewed **annually**. Retained for 7 years.
- **Executive Risk Presentations:** Materials presented to board and executive leadership showing risk posture, trends, and strategic recommendations. Presented **monthly**. Retained for 5 years.

### N.4 Risk Mitigation Evidence
- **Risk Mitigation Plans:** Detailed action plans for high and critical risks showing specific actions, responsible parties, target completion dates, resource allocation, and success metrics. Created **per risk** with **monthly status updates**. Retained for 5 years.
- **Control Implementation Records:** Documentation of security controls implemented to mitigate identified risks, including implementation date, testing results, and effectiveness assessment. Retained for 5 years.
- **Remediation Tracking:** ServiceNow tickets showing mitigation action status, completion dates, and verification of effectiveness. Retained for 5 years.

### N.5 Risk Monitoring Evidence
- **Risk Monitoring Dashboards:** Real-time visualization of key risk indicators, mitigation progress, risk trends, and heat maps exported **monthly**. Retained for 3 years.
- **Key Risk Indicator (KRI) Reports:** Trending analysis of leading indicators that signal changes in risk exposure with threshold alerts. Generated **monthly**. Retained for 3 years.
- **Continuous Monitoring Logs:** Automated monitoring system outputs showing risk-related events and threshold breaches. Retained for 1 year.
- **Risk Trend Analysis:** Quarterly analysis of risk posture changes, emerging risks, and mitigation effectiveness. Retained for 5 years.

### N.6 Audit and Assessment Evidence
- **Internal Audit Reports:** Annual internal risk management program audits showing findings, severity classifications, remediation plans, and closure evidence. Retained for 7 years.
- **External Audit Reports:** Third-party assessments of risk management program maturity and compliance. Retained for 7 years.
- **Control Effectiveness Assessments:** Documentation of control testing results with ratings (Effective, Partially Effective, Ineffective) and remediation requirements. Retained for 5 years.
- **Incident Correlation Analysis:** Analysis connecting realized incidents to previously identified risks, validating risk assessment accuracy. Completed **per incident**. Retained for 7 years.

### N.7 Training and Awareness Evidence
- **Risk Training Records:** Learning management system (LMS) reports showing employee name, course title, completion date, and pass/fail status for risk awareness training. Retained for 3 years.
- **Risk Owner Certification:** Documentation of specialized training for risk owners on assessment methodology, treatment planning, and monitoring requirements. Retained for 3 years.
- **Policy Acknowledgements:** Signed attestations (digital or physical) confirming employees have read, understood, and agree to comply with risk management policies. Retained for 3 years.

### N.8 Third-Party Risk Evidence
- **Vendor Risk Assessments:** Due diligence questionnaires, security reviews, and risk ratings for vendors handling sensitive data or critical processes. Retained for duration of contract + 3 years.
- **Third-Party Risk Register:** Dedicated tracking of vendor and supply chain risks with owner assignment and treatment plans. Retained for contract duration + 3 years.
- **Vendor Performance Reviews:** Annual reviews of vendor risk posture and compliance with contractual risk management obligations. Retained for 5 years.
