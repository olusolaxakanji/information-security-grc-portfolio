## Description

This policy defines acceptable and prohibited uses of Emyzer Technology information systems, networks, and data to protect organizational assets and ensure responsible use of technology resources.

## A. Purpose

To establish clear guidelines for the acceptable use of Emyzer Technology information systems, networks, devices, and data. This policy defines user responsibilities, permitted activities, and prohibited behaviors to protect organizational assets, maintain system integrity, ensure regulatory compliance, and preserve the organization's reputation. All users are expected to exercise good judgment and use technology resources in a professional, ethical, and lawful manner.

---

## B. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, interns, and authorized third parties who access Emyzer Technology information systems, networks, or data. It encompasses all technology resources owned, leased, or managed by Emyzer Technology, including:

- Desktop computers, laptops, and mobile devices
- Servers, networks, and cloud infrastructure
- Email systems and collaboration platforms
- Internet access and web applications
- Telecommunications systems
- Removable media and storage devices
- Software and applications (licensed and developed)
- Data in all forms (electronic, printed, verbal)

**Third party applicability:** Third parties granted access to organizational systems shall comply with this policy as a condition of access. Contractual agreements shall reference this policy and require equivalent controls.

## C. Definitions

1. **Information Systems:** Hardware, software, networks, and data used to create, process, store, or transmit information for business purposes.

2. **Authorized User:** An individual granted access to Emyzer Technology information systems through a formal approval process based on business need.

3. **Personal Use:** Use of organizational technology resources for non business purposes, including personal communications, web browsing, and file storage.

4. **Prohibited Activity:** Any use of information systems that violates this policy, applicable laws, or organizational standards.

5. **Monitoring:** The observation, recording, and analysis of user activities on information systems for security, compliance, and performance purposes.

6. **Bring Your Own Device (BYOD):** The practice of employees using personal devices to access organizational systems and data.

## D. Policy Statement

Emyzer Technology provides information systems and technology resources to support business operations and enable workforce productivity. Users are granted access to these resources as a privilege, not a right, and are expected to use them responsibly. To achieve this, the organization shall:

1. Require all users to acknowledge and comply with this policy as a condition of system access.

2. Permit use of information systems **primarily for business purposes**, with limited personal use allowed subject to restrictions defined in this policy.

3. Prohibit any use that violates laws, regulations, contractual obligations, or organizational policies.

4. Monitor user activities on information systems to ensure compliance, detect security threats, and investigate incidents. **Users have no expectation of privacy when using organizational systems.**

5. Require users to protect organizational data according to its classification level and handle all information with appropriate care.

6. Hold users accountable for all activities performed under their credentials and require immediate reporting of suspected compromise.

7. Reserve the right to revoke access, investigate violations, and take disciplinary action for policy non compliance.

8. Review and update this policy **annually** to address evolving threats, technologies, and regulatory requirements.

## E. Acceptable Use Requirements

### E.1 General Use Requirements

All users shall:

1. Use information systems only for authorized business purposes and limited approved personal use.

2. Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and organizational policies when using technology resources.

3. Protect login credentials and never share passwords, tokens, or access badges with others.

4. Lock or log off workstations when leaving them unattended, even briefly.

5. Report security incidents, policy violations, or suspicious activities **within 1 hour** of discovery.

6. Complete security awareness training **within 30 days** of hire and **annually** thereafter.

7. Cooperate with security investigations and provide information when requested by authorized personnel.

8. Use only approved software and refrain from installing unauthorized applications on organizational devices.

### E.2 Data Protection Requirements

All users shall:

1. Handle data according to its classification level (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted) as defined in the Data Classification Policy.

2. Store Confidential and Restricted data only on approved systems with appropriate security controls.

3. Encrypt sensitive data before transmission outside the corporate network using approved methods.

4. Never store organizational data on unauthorized cloud services, personal devices (unless BYOD approved), or removable media without explicit approval.

5. Dispose of data securely according to disposal requirements for its classification level.

6. Report suspected data breaches or unauthorized disclosures **immediately** upon discovery.

### E.3 Email and Communications Requirements

All users shall:

1. Use organizational email and messaging systems professionally and in accordance with business standards.

2. Never send Confidential or Restricted data via email unless encrypted using approved tools.

3. Exercise caution with email attachments and links to avoid phishing and malware threats.

4. Include appropriate classification labels in email subject lines when transmitting classified information.

5. Use organizational communication systems for business purposes; personal use shall be minimal and not interfere with work.

6. Never use organizational email to send offensive, discriminatory, harassing, or defamatory content.

7. Retain business communications according to the organization's records retention schedule.

### E.4 Internet and Web Use Requirements

All users shall:

1. Use internet access primarily for business purposes; limited personal use is permitted during breaks if it does not impact productivity or violate this policy.

2. Never access websites containing illegal, offensive, discriminatory, or sexually explicit content.

3. Never download or install software without IT approval and verification of licensing compliance.

4. Never use organizational internet access to conduct personal business ventures or external employment activities.

5. Exercise caution when providing organizational information on websites and verify legitimacy before entering credentials.

6. Report suspicious websites or potential security threats to the IT Security Team.

### E.5 Device and Equipment Requirements

All users shall:

1. Use organizational devices (laptops, mobile phones, tablets) primarily for business purposes.

2. Physically secure devices when not in use, especially when traveling or working remotely.

3. Never leave devices unattended in vehicles, public spaces, or unsecured locations.

4. Report lost or stolen devices **within 4 hours** of discovery to enable remote wipe and access revocation.

5. Allow IT to install security software, updates, and patches on organizational devices.

6. Return all organizational devices and data upon termination of employment or contract.

### E.6 BYOD Requirements

Users who access organizational systems from personal devices shall:

1. Obtain approval from their manager and IT before connecting personal devices to organizational systems.

2. Install required security software (MDM agent, antivirus) on personal devices used for work.

3. Enable device encryption and screen lock with PIN, password, or biometric authentication.

4. Allow remote wipe of organizational data from personal devices if the device is lost, stolen, or upon termination.

5. Keep personal devices updated with current operating system and security patches.

6. Never store Restricted data on personal devices without explicit CISO approval.

## F. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on Emyzer Technology information systems:

### F.1 Security Violations

1. Attempting to bypass, disable, or circumvent security controls, firewalls, or access restrictions.

2. Using another person's credentials or allowing others to use your credentials.

3. Unauthorized access to systems, data, or networks, including attempts to exploit vulnerabilities.

4. Installing or running hacking tools, port scanners, or network sniffers without authorization.

5. Intentionally introducing malware, viruses, or malicious code into organizational systems.

6. Unauthorized interception, monitoring, or recording of network traffic or communications.

### F.2 Data Violations

1. Unauthorized access, copying, modification, or deletion of organizational data.

2. Sharing Confidential or Restricted data with unauthorized individuals, including family members or friends.

3. Transferring organizational data to unauthorized external systems, personal accounts, or cloud services.

4. Removing or disabling data classification labels or security markings.

5. Violating data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA, PCI DSS, HIPAA) through improper data handling.

### F.3 Illegal and Unethical Activities

1. Using organizational systems for any illegal purpose or to facilitate illegal activities.

2. Accessing, downloading, storing, or distributing illegal content, including pirated software or media.

3. Engaging in harassment, discrimination, bullying, or threatening behavior via organizational systems.

4. Sending or forwarding offensive, defamatory, or discriminatory content.

5. Using organizational systems to conduct unauthorized commercial activities or personal business ventures.

6. Gambling or participating in illegal betting activities using organizational resources.

### F.4 Resource Misuse

1. Excessive personal use that interferes with job responsibilities or degrades system performance.

2. Using organizational systems to send spam, chain letters, or unauthorized mass communications.

3. Consuming excessive bandwidth through streaming, large downloads, or peer to peer file sharing for personal purposes.

4. Cryptocurrency mining or other resource intensive personal computing activities.

5. Using organizational systems to store excessive personal files, photos, or media.

### F.5 Intellectual Property Violations

1. Installing, copying, or distributing software in violation of licensing agreements.

2. Sharing proprietary organizational information with competitors or unauthorized parties.

3. Downloading or distributing copyrighted materials without proper authorization.

4. Using organizational resources to develop intellectual property for personal benefit or external parties.

## G. Monitoring and Privacy

### G.1 Monitoring Notice

Emyzer Technology reserves the right to monitor, log, access, and review all activities on organizational information systems without prior notice. Monitoring may include:

- Email content, attachments, and metadata
- Internet browsing history and web application usage
- File access, creation, modification, and deletion
- Login/logout activities and session durations
- Network traffic and bandwidth utilization
- Instant messaging and collaboration platform communications
- Phone calls and voicemail on organizational telecommunications systems
- Physical access logs and CCTV footage in secure areas

### G.2 Privacy Expectations

Users acknowledge and accept the following:

1. **No expectation of privacy** exists when using organizational information systems, networks, or devices.

2. Personal communications conducted on organizational systems may be accessed during investigations or audits.

3. Monitoring data may be retained and used as evidence in disciplinary proceedings or legal actions.

4. The organization may access user accounts without notice when required for security, legal, or business continuity purposes.

### G.3 Monitoring Governance

1. Monitoring activities shall be conducted by authorized personnel only (IT Security, HR, Legal, Internal Audit).

2. Access to monitoring data shall be restricted to individuals with a legitimate business need.

3. Monitoring shall be conducted in compliance with applicable privacy laws and regulations.

4. Monitoring findings shall be documented and retained according to the Evidence of Compliance requirements.

## H. Roles and Responsibilities

### H.1 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Provides executive oversight of the acceptable use program. Shall approve policy exceptions exceeding 30 days, strategic policy changes, and escalated disciplinary recommendations. Shall review policy effectiveness **annually** as part of ISMS management review.

### H.2 Information Security Officer (ISO)
Shall develop, implement, and maintain the acceptable use framework under CISO direction. Shall approve tactical exceptions (30 days or less), review monitoring reports **monthly**, and coordinate investigations of policy violations. Shall recommend policy updates based on incident trends and emerging threats.

### H.3 IT Department
Shall implement technical controls to enforce acceptable use requirements. Shall monitor user activities, maintain audit logs, and provide monitoring reports to the ISO **monthly**. Shall provision and deprovision user access according to defined SLAs. Shall investigate technical aspects of policy violations.

### H.4 Human Resources
Shall ensure policy acknowledgement is obtained during onboarding and **annually** thereafter. Shall coordinate with ISO on disciplinary actions for policy violations. Shall maintain employee training and acknowledgement records. Shall participate in investigations involving employee misconduct.

### H.5 Department Managers
Shall ensure team members understand and comply with acceptable use requirements. Shall approve or deny personal device (BYOD) requests from team members. Shall report policy violations within their teams **within 24 hours** of discovery. Shall support investigations and enforce disciplinary actions.

### H.6 All Users
Shall read, understand, and comply with this policy. Shall complete security awareness training **within 30 days** of hire and **annually** thereafter. Shall acknowledge this policy **annually** via the designated system. Shall report policy violations, security incidents, or suspicious activities **within 1 hour** of discovery.

## I. Compliance and Enforcement

### I.1 Monitoring and Audits

Compliance with this policy shall be monitored through:

- **Continuous technical monitoring** of user activities on information systems
- **Monthly reviews** of monitoring data and security alerts by the ISO
- **Quarterly audits** of user access and activity patterns
- **Annual policy compliance assessments** as part of ISMS audits

### I.2 Violation Response

Policy violations shall be addressed based on severity:

| Severity | Examples | Response |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Minor** | Occasional excessive personal use; failure to lock workstation | Verbal warning; documented counseling |
| **Moderate** | Sharing credentials; unauthorized software installation; repeated minor violations | Written warning; mandatory training; temporary access restriction |
| **Major** | Unauthorized data access; circumventing security controls; harassment | Suspension of access; formal investigation; disciplinary action up to termination |
| **Critical** | Intentional data theft; malware introduction; illegal activity | Immediate access revocation; termination; legal action; law enforcement referral |

### I.3 Investigation Process

1. The ISO shall initiate investigations upon report of suspected violations.
2. IT shall preserve relevant logs, files, and evidence.
3. HR shall participate in investigations involving employee conduct.
4. Legal shall be consulted for violations involving potential legal exposure.
5. Findings shall be documented and retained for **7 years**.
6. Users shall cooperate fully with investigations; failure to cooperate is itself a violation.

## J. Policy Exceptions

### J.1 Exception Request Process
Users requiring temporary deviation from this policy shall:
1. Submit exception requests via **ServiceNow GRC workflow** with detailed business justification, risk assessment, and proposed compensating controls.
2. Obtain approval from the Information Security Officer for exceptions of 30 days or less, or CISO for exceptions exceeding 30 days.
3. Accept exceptions valid for a maximum of **90 days** unless formally renewed.
4. Implement and document compensating controls during the exception period.

### J.2 Exception Governance
- All active exceptions shall be reviewed **quarterly** by the Information Security Officer.
- Exception renewals shall require updated business justification and risk assessment.
- Each exception shall include a remediation plan with a target closure date not exceeding **180 days** from initial approval.
- Exception tracking shall be maintained in the GRC platform with status updates provided to executive leadership **monthly**.

### P.1 Acknowledgement Evidence
- **Policy Acknowledgement Records:** Digital or signed acknowledgements confirming users have read, understood, and agree to comply with this policy. Collected during onboarding and annually thereafter. Retained for duration of employment plus 3 years.
- **New Hire Acknowledgements:** Evidence of policy acknowledgement completed within first week of employment. Retained for duration of employment plus 3 years.
- **Annual Re-acknowledgement Records:** Documentation of annual policy acknowledgement campaigns, including completion rates and follow-up actions for non-responders. Retained for 3 years.

### P.2 Training Evidence
- **Training Completion Records:** LMS reports showing employee name, acceptable use training course, completion date, and assessment score. Retained for 3 years.
- **New Hire Training Records:** Evidence of security awareness training (including acceptable use) completed within 30 days of start date. Retained for 3 years.
- **Remedial Training Records:** Documentation of mandatory training assigned following policy violations, including completion verification. Retained for 3 years.

### P.3 Monitoring Evidence
- **System Activity Logs:** Logs of user authentication, file access, email, and internet activity from SIEM and monitoring tools. Retained for 1 year.
- **Monthly Monitoring Reports:** ISO reviews of monitoring data, security alerts, and anomalous activity patterns. Retained for 3 years.
- **DLP Alerts:** Records of data loss prevention alerts, including user, action, data involved, and resolution. Retained for 3 years.

### P.4 Violation and Investigation Evidence
- **Incident Reports:** Documentation of reported policy violations, including reporter, date, description, and initial assessment. Retained for 7 years.
- **Investigation Files:** Complete investigation records including evidence collected, interviews conducted, findings, and recommendations. Retained for 7 years.
- **Disciplinary Action Records:** Documentation of warnings, suspensions, terminations, and other disciplinary actions resulting from violations. Retained for duration of employment plus 7 years.

### P.5 Device Management Evidence
- **BYOD Enrollment Records:** Approved BYOD requests, device registration, and security configuration verification. Retained for device enrollment period plus 3 years.
- **Lost/Stolen Device Reports:** Records of reported lost or stolen devices, response actions taken, and remote wipe confirmations. Retained for 5 years.
- **Device Return Records:** Documentation of device return upon termination, including data wipe verification. Retained for 3 years.

### P.6 Exception and Audit Evidence
- **Exception Records:** Approved exception requests with business justification, risk assessment, compensating controls, approval chain, and expiration date. Retained for exception duration plus 3 years.
- **Quarterly Audit Reports:** Audit findings assessing user compliance with acceptable use requirements, violation trends, and remediation tracking. Retained for 5 years.
- **Annual Compliance Assessments:** Results of annual acceptable use policy compliance reviews as part of ISMS audits. Retained for 5 years.
