## A. Purpose

To ensure Emyzer Technology's preparedness to respond to and recover from incidents and disruptions, minimizing the impact on business operations, assets, and employees. This policy aligns with ISO 22301, ISO/IEC 27031, and the Business Continuity Institute's Good Practice Guidelines (BCI GPG).

## C. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Business Continuity (BC)** | The capability of the organization to continue delivery of products or services at acceptable predefined levels following a disruptive incident. |
| **Disaster Recovery (DR)** | The process, policies, and procedures related to preparing for recovery or continuation of technology infrastructure critical to an organization after a natural or human-induced disaster. |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event that has the potential to disrupt business operations. |
| **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)** | The maximum acceptable length of time that can elapse before the lack of a business function severely impacts the organization. |
| **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)** | The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time. |
| **Business Impact Analysis (BIA)** | A process that identifies and evaluates the potential effects of disruptions to critical business operations. |

---

## D. Policy Statement

Emyzer Technology adheres to robust business continuity and disaster recovery capabilities to ensure resilience against disruptions. The organization will:

1. **Develop, implement, and maintain** BC and DR plans based on risk assessments and business impact analyses.
2. **Regularly test and update** these plans to reflect changing business conditions and technology advancements.
3. **Ensure all employees understand** their roles and responsibilities during a disruption.
4. **Align BC and DR activities** with ISO 22301, ISO/IEC 27031, and BCI GPG standards.
5. **Establish and monitor** Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) for critical functions.

## F. Procedures

### F.1 Risk Assessment
Conduct regular risk assessments to identify potential threats and vulnerabilities. Document findings and prioritize risks based on likelihood and impact.

### F.2 Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
Perform BIAs to determine the criticality of business functions and establish RTOs and RPOs. Update BIA documentation annually or when significant changes occur.

### F.3 Plan Development
Create comprehensive BC and DR plans based on risk assessments and BIAs, incorporating necessary resources, processes, and recovery strategies. Maintain version control and distribution records.

### F.4 Testing and Maintenance
Conduct regular drills and simulations to test the effectiveness of BC and DR plans. Update plans as needed based on test outcomes and organizational changes.

### F.5 Training and Awareness
Provide ongoing training and awareness programs to ensure all employees understand their roles and responsibilities in executing BC and DR plans.

### F.6 Plan Activation and Recovery
Establish clear activation criteria and escalation procedures. Document recovery steps and communication protocols for incident response.

## G. Compliance and Monitoring

### Internal Audits
Conduct regular internal audits to ensure adherence to the BC and DR policy and identify areas for improvement.

### Performance Metrics
Establish and monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) to assess the effectiveness of BC and DR initiatives, including RTO/RPO achievement rates.

### Reporting
Regularly report on BC and DR activities to executive management, including test results, incidents, and improvement recommendations.
